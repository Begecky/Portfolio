The continued bio...

I currently work as a data scientist for a local non-profit consulting group, assisting with data management, analysis and general research. Prior to this position, I completed a fair amount of research during my graduate studies. Some of this research was overseas, in musty old libraries with manuscripts older than our country and some of it was stateside, oftentimes at the Library of Congress. While I ultimately decided to see what jobs fell outside of the academic realm, I knew I wanted to find something where I could continue using my research skills.

I entertained the idea of going to law-school for quite awhile, but also considered some development bootcamps since I always had a concurrent interest in tech. Once I found the existence of data-science bootcamps, I was sold. I enjoyed learning the material with Thinkful and working on the various projects throughout the curriculum. More often than not, I find the combination of wrangling, analysis, research, visualization and problem-solving to be fascinating. Using data, statistics and code to assess a given situation and sift out potential solutions seems like a career which is right up my alley.

Outside of work, I enjoy hanging out with friends in the mountains of Colorado (either on horseback, on skiis or a mountainbike). More recently, my outdoor activities have included keeping the particular feline at your right, from getting into trouble with the critters below (although he has protected himself thus far). I can also be found knitting or building an automated green house.
